Bolted Joint – Features and functions

A disciplined Torque Flange Assembly (TFA) workflow designed for zero leaks and verified compliance.

Phase 1

Flange Scope Preparation

Comprehensive planning and asset identification. Defining specifications, engineering requirements, and joint categorization before field execution begins.

Phase 2

Break Flange & Inspection

Controlled disassembly with professional status tracking. Inspecting flange faces, bolts, and nut surfaces to ensure full integrity before re-assembly.

Phase 3

Prepare Flange & Tightening

Executing controlled torque or tensioning. Utilizing QR-code verification for lubricants, gaskets, and tools to meet precise engineering values.

Phase 4

Quality Control & Assurance

Final sign-off with evidence verification. Generation of the joint ‘Birth Certificate’ and updating the live project status dashboard.
